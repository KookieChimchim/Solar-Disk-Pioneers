Exploring the Mysteries

The sun has been quite a mystery and topic of research since the young days of the space race. Many spacecrafts either tried or somewhat helped in knowing the sun better as of now.

1960 to 1969 NASA launched 6 pioneer spacecrafts orbiter types out of which 5 are considered successfull 5 of the 6 were launched in the later part of decade spacecrafts being pioneer 5, 6A , 7B , 8C , 9D, E out of these pioneer 5 being the first launched measured magnetic field phenomena , solar flare particles , ionization in the planetary region over the course of 1965 to 1968 4 successfull pioneer missions were launched to study network of solar - orbiting "space weather" monitors , observing solar wind , cosmic rays and magnetic fields in 1969- The year when mankind made a giant leap to the Moon- was the failure in Solar Mission Pioneer E which intended to be a part of pioneer 6-9 network but it failed to reach the orbit.

2nd wave of solar missions started from 1974 to 1997 during this period 7 spacecrafts were launched all being orbiter types. This was the first time some other space agency colaborated with NASA on solar probes helios series of solar probes helios a and helios b was a collaborated project of nasa and german space agency dfvlr they both were a success and took observations of solar winds , magnetic and electric fields , cosmic rays and cosmic dusts between Earth and Sun.

ISEE -3 NASA Loner Orbital Mission in 1978 was also a success which observed solar phenomena in conjunction with earth-orbiting ISEE-1 and ISEE-2; later renamed International Cometary Explorer (ICE) and directed to Comet Giacobini-Zinner ISEE-1 and ISEE-2 remained near the Earth. ISEE-3 was the first spacecraft to be placed in a halo orbit at the Earth-Sun Lagrangian points L1 and it was later launched into a heliocentric orbit.next in series was a nasa esa collaborative Ulysses which was a 3 pass orbiter program with 3 passes spreade over course of time first pass from 1994 and 1995 which made south and north polar observations in respective years same happened in the second and third pass that is 2000 and 2001 for 2nd pass and 2007 and 2008 for the 3rd it was a huge success as it gave key information of Ulysses’ principal findings included data that showed there is a weakening of the solar wind over time (which was at a 50-year low in 2008).

From the mission indicated the solar magnetic field at the Sun's poles is much weaker than previously assumed. Ulysses helped determine that the Sun’s magnetic field “reverses” in direction every 11 years.
